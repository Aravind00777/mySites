I want to LIVE

Could Brintellix’s tolerability profile help adult patients like Samantha who are struggling to stay on medication due to side effects?1-3

Fictional patients used for illustrative purposes.

After Samantha started taking an SSRI at the start of this year, she found that it was affecting her sex drive,² leading her to feel disconnected from her husband Robert.

It’s important for Samantha to find a treatment for her MDD that doesn’t affect her intimacy with her husband.

Sexual dysfunction:

Sexual dysfunction is common with serotonergic antidepressants, including SSRIs and SNRIs, and does not resolve in most patients²

MDD, major depressive disorder; SNRIs, serotonin–norepinephrine reuptake inhibitors;

Brintellix’s low rate of sexual dysfunction (5-15 mg* vs. placebo)¹³ may help patients like Samantha who are experiencing sexual dysfunction with their current treatment²

*Brintellix 15 mg is not available for use in the UK. The 20 mg dose of Brintellix was associated with an increase in sexual dysfunction compared with placebo (an incidence difference of 14.2%)1

Brintellix demonstrates a low-rate of patient-reported sexual dysfunction at 5-15 mg* vs. placebo1,¹³

  • Patient-reported sexual dysfunction with Brintellix was low, and similar to that in the placebo group during short-term treatment (1.6-1.8% vs 1.0%)*3
  • *Brintellix 15 mg is not available for use in the UK. The 20 mg dose of Brintellix was associated with an increase in sexual dysfunction compared to placebo (an incidence difference of 14.2%) 1

For further information about Brintellix, including Tolerability, Special Warnings and Precautions and Contraindications, please visit the About Brintellix Section

Brintellix demonstrates greater improvement in SSRI-induced treatment-emergent sexual dysfunction compared with escitalopram²

  • Switching from an SSRI** to Brintellix was associated with significant clinical improvements in sexual functioning in patients diagnosed with MDD experiencing SSRI-related sexual dysfunction²
  • Improvements in sexual functioning with Brintellix were significantly greater than those with escitalopram at week 8 (p< 0.05) as measured by change from baseline in the CSFQ-14 total score²
**citalopram, paroxetine, or sertraline
CSFQ-14, Changes in Sexual Functioning Questionnaire 14 items; SSRIs, selective serotonin reuptake inhibitors.

Jacobsen PL et al. 2015¹

A randomised, double-blind study of Brintellix vs. escitalopram on sexual functioning in patients with major depressive disorder (MDD) experiencing treatment-emergent sexual dysfunction.

Study objective: Compare the effects of Brintellix vs. escitalopram on sexual functioning in adults with well-treated major depressive disorder experiencing SSRI-induced sexual dysfunction.
Rotate your device for a larger view of the study design
Eight-week double-blind treatment period
Primary outcome: Change from baseline in the CSFQ-14 total score after eight weeks of treatment.
Secondary outcomes: The MADRS, CGI, and POMS-brief were used to assess antidepressant efficacy. Safety was assessed via adverse events, vital signs, electrocardiograms, laboratory values, weight, and physical examination findings.
CGI-S, Clinical Global Impression - Severity; CSFQ-14, Change in Sexual Functioning Questionnaire 14 items; MADRS, Montgomery-Åsberg Depression Rating Scale; MDE, major depressive episode; POMS-brief, Profile of Mood States brief form; SSRI, selective serotonin reuptake inhibitor.
